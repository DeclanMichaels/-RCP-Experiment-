RCP Explanation Probe Analysis

630 responses across 5 models and 7 framings

Key findings from the systematic analysis of 525+ explanation probes. Each model produced one-sentence explanations for 15 moral concept pairs under 7 framing conditions.

Mean Preamble ROUGE-1 Recall

mean ROUGE-1 recall of preamble vocabulary across all non-neutral framings. GPT-4o highest (0.34), Gemini-Flash lowest (0.08).

Epistemic Hedges Under Nonsense

mean epistemic hedge count under nonsense framing vs 0.6-1.1 under neutral (Hyland, 1998). Models assert absurd frameworks with more certainty than real ones.

Lowest Jaccard Overlap

mean Jaccard between neutral and nonsense vocabularies, confirming models generate genuinely novel content rather than templating.

Response Length Increase

mean word increase from neutral to framed responses. Models elaborate more when given a frame to perform.

Preamble ROUGE-1 Recall (Lin, 2004): Fraction of the framing preamble's content vocabulary that appears in the model's response. Higher values indicate the model absorbed more of the preamble's language into its explanation. Stopwords excluded. Neutral is always 0.00 (no preamble to recall).
Epistemic Hedges (Hyland, 1998): Average number of standard linguistic hedging devices per response — modal auxiliaries ("may," "might," "could"), epistemic verbs ("seem," "suggest," "appear"), and modal adverbs ("often," "generally," "typically"). The drop to near-zero under nonsense framing is a compliance signature: models express more certainty about absurd frameworks than real ones.
Relational Boilerplate: Average count of vague connective phrases per response ("interconnected," "intertwined," "complementary," "fundamentally," "inextricably"). These are not epistemic hedges — they don't express uncertainty — but formulaic relational language common in LLM outputs. Also drops to zero under nonsense framing.
Perspective Adoption Rate: Fraction of responses containing explicit perspective-taking markers ("From this perspective," "In this society," "From our angular framework"). Reveals distinct compliance styles across models.
Mean Response Length (words): Models consistently produce longer explanations under framing conditions than under neutral prompting, suggesting elaboration is driven by the framing context rather than the concept pair.
Jaccard Overlap with Neutral: Mean word-set similarity between neutral and each framing, averaged across concept pairs. Lower values mean more distinct vocabulary. Nonsense framing consistently produces the most novel vocabulary.
